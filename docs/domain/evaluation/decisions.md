# Evaluation Decisions

## Ambiguity Resolutions

- "Handoff trigger configuration" could mean hard-coded only, business-configurable, or prompt-defined. Resolved: V1 keeps fixed baseline triggers and permits Agent Contract or policy configuration only for enterprise high-value failure scenarios; frontend and prompt-defined triggers are not trusted.
- "Admit an exactly deduplicated candidate" could mean boosting confidence because multiple sources returned it, averaging scores, selecting the highest score, or applying conservative admission. Resolved: **Merged Evidence Admission Evaluation** keeps WRRF contribution aggregation separate from admission; an approved scorer evaluates the merged chunk once when configured, otherwise the minimum available calibrated contributor score applies, and candidates with no valid score remain inadmissible.
- "Historical insurance consultation logs" could mean an immediately usable benchmark or raw input to curation. Resolved: the available logs have no confirmed expected answers or citations and therefore remain uncurated production samples; they become Evaluation Cases only through **Evaluation Curation**, including redaction, deduplication, the 30/50/20 insurance query-type labels, expected-resolution and rule-basis annotation, risk classification, and Domain Evaluation Reviewer plus Harness Evaluation Reviewer confirmation.
- "Use historical insurance logs for evaluation" could mean unrestricted replay or authorized redacted curation. Resolved: approved logs may be processed only inside the Private Knowledge Processing Boundary after deterministic sensitive-field removal and data minimization; model-assisted prelabeling sees only the redacted sample, and promotion still requires Evaluation Curation Review Permission and human confirmation.
- "Initial insurance Knowledge benchmark size" could mean a small smoke list or a production-shaped evaluation cohort. Resolved: curate 500 human-confirmed cases as the first milestone and expand toward 1,000; preserve the 30/50/20 query-type profile, separate tuning cases from an unseen acceptance set, and require rule-version, citation, authority-gate, warning, clarification, conflict, or refusal labels as applicable.
- "Use the 500 cases for model selection and release" could mean tuning and accepting against the same visible set. Resolved: the **Insurance Knowledge Gold Suite** contains 300 visible tuning cases and 200 access-controlled unseen acceptance cases; the sealed cohort alone authorizes release, while a separate 100-to-200-sample parsing benchmark covers layout, table, OCR, and citation-coordinate fidelity.
- "Rerun the unseen 200 cases after every change" could mean repeatedly probing a static holdout or evaluating one frozen candidate under controlled custody. Resolved: **Sealed Knowledge Acceptance Evaluation** permits one acceptance execution per frozen candidate, withholds item-level feedback from tuners, moves any revealed failed case into the visible cohort, and replenishes the unseen cohort before a later acceptance decision; tuning iterations use only the 300 visible cases.
- "Pass Knowledge evaluation" could mean meeting one blended quality score or satisfying independent release gates. Resolved: the **Insurance Knowledge Hard Release Gate** is non-compensating and requires zero authorization leakage, wrong-version or wrong-precedence use, unresolvable citations, advice under authority uncertainty, and high-severity unsupported conclusions; retrieval and ordinary answer-quality thresholds apply only after those hard conditions pass.
- "Set retrieval acceptance thresholds" could mean optimizing only final-answer scores or measuring evidence availability directly. Resolved: the initial target is required-evidence Recall@50 of at least 95 percent overall and at least 90 percent in every query-type slice, plus complete-evidence Top-10 coverage of at least 90 percent for conditional-guidance and comparison cases; these provisional quality thresholds may rise after baseline measurement but cannot weaken the hard release gate.

## Relationship And Reference Notes

- Evaluation verifies governed behavior but does not become runtime authority.

- Published Agent Version boundaries matter because evaluation trends must be version-aware.

- **Workflow Template Stages** are reserved for Agent-owner-visible configuration, explanation, audit, or evaluation points; internal policy checks, validators, model requests, helper functions, and Runtime Plane nodes are not automatically stages.
- **Merged Evidence Admission Evaluation** does not reward duplicate retrieval hits with a higher Evidence Admission Score. When configured, an approved admission scorer evaluates the merged normalized chunk once; otherwise the merged candidate uses the minimum available calibrated Evidence Admission Score from contributing sources. Contributors without calibrated admission scores remain visible in Trace but do not participate in score aggregation, and a merged candidate with no valid admission score remains inadmissible.
